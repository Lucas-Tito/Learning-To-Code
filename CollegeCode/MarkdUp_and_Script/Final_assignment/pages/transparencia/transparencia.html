Transparência

Podemos tentar resumir transparência como um atributo da comunicação que tenta permitir que stakeholders entendam o projeto de software pela divulgação da informação. Transparência foi conceituada em termos de divulgação de informação ou de processo (Leite and Cappelli, 2010). Pelo ponto de vista de software, informação transparente significa tornar transparente a informação que o software lida (do Prado Leite and Cappelli, 2008). (Fiquei com preguiça de traduzir o resto)Process transparency, on the other hand, means the software can reveal how it works, what it does, and how it does it (do Prado Leite and Cappelli, 2008). Other studies define transparency as follows: Transparency can be defined as the open flow of information (Holzner and Holzner, 2006) and the release of information by institutions that is relevant to evalu- ating these institutions (Florini, 2000).

In the context of Software Management, transparency is an important property, but also surprisingly understudied. Besides, transparency is an umbrella term and can be used in different ways. As stated previously, transparency isn’t a well studied prop- erty, so there are not many paths to systematically introduce transparency in software development.
